# Database Owner Understanding & Architecture Notes

## 1. Architectural Classification (Data Layering)
To ensure scalability and clean separation of concerns, the 9 minimum tables are structured into three distinct logical architectural layers:

### A. Staging Layer (Raw Data Landing)
* **Tables:** `sources`, `documents`
* **Purpose:** Acts as an immutable entry point. Data is stored here in its original state with minimal transformations to prevent data loss and ensure reproducibility.

### B. Core Layer (Structured & Semantic Store)
* **Tables:** `document_chunks`, `structured_records`
* **Purpose:** The optimized operational data store. Text is chunked and vectorized (`pgvector`) for AI tasks, and relational data is normalized/cleaned for analytical queries.

### C. Analytics & Logging Layer (System & Product Insights)
* **Tables:** `ingestion_logs`, `pipeline_runs`, `analytics_events`, `rag_query_logs`, `prediction_logs`
* **Purpose:** Monitors system health, data quality, and user interaction. These tables support audit trails and feed BI dashboards (Power BI).

## 2. Week 1 Assessment Questions

### Q1: What is your role in the platform?
* **Answer:** My role is the **Database, Quality, and Analytics Owner**. I am responsible for designing the PostgreSQL schema (including `pgvector`), maintaining data integrity, setting up quality checks, and preparing optimized SQL views ready for Power BI dashboards and reports.

### Q2: What input does your module need?
* **Answer:** My module needs raw operational data, parsed texts, and execution metadata/logs generated by the **Integration & Ingestion Layer** (ETL/ELT pipelines), as well as generated vectors from the **Processing & AI Layer**.

### Q3: What output will your module produce?
* **Answer:** A structured, indexed, and reliable database schema, relational tables, specialized vector columns, optimized query views, and performance logs.

### Q4: Which team will use your output?
* **Answer:** * The **Processing & AI Team** will consume the `document_chunks` (vector embeddings) for RAG and `structured_records` for training/evaluation.
  * The **Application Team** will use my SQL/Analytics views to populate the Dashboards and Reports.

### Q5: What are your main tools?
* **Answer:** PostgreSQL (Core DBMS), `pgvector` (Vector Extension), DBeaver(Database Management GUI), and SQL (Structured Query Language).
